## THE MECHANISM (Todd Brown "Big Idea" Framework)

Every business that does outbound sales has the same bottleneck: the SDR.

They're expensive ($4,500-$7,000/mo). They're inconsistent (good month, bad month, quit). They can only do one thing at a time — they can't call while they email while they text. And they definitely can't do 200 prospects a day with deep personalization on every touchpoint.

**The breakthrough isn't better SDRs. It's a different species of SDR.**

We deploy a 5-Agent AI SDR Team that operates across three channels simultaneously — email, phone, and SMS — with priority scoring that focuses your best resources on your best prospects.

**The 3 innovations that make this work:**

### Innovation 1: Priority Scoring (A/B/C Tiering)
Not every lead deserves a phone call. The Prospector agent scores every lead against your ICP and assigns a tier:
- **A-Leads (top 20%):** Get the full sequence — email + phone call + SMS
- **B-Leads (middle 50%):** Get email + SMS
- **C-Leads (bottom 30%):** Get email only

This reduces cost per meeting by 40% versus uniform outreach. You stop wasting phone calls on C-leads and start focusing calls where they convert.

### Innovation 2: Speed-to-Lead Triggers
Here's the stat that changes everything: **calling a prospect within 5 minutes of them opening your email yields a 10x higher connect rate than calling the next day.**

When an A-lead opens your email, the Phone SDR agent calls them immediately. Not tomorrow. Not "during the next calling block." Within 5 minutes.

No human SDR can do this. They're in a meeting. They're at lunch. They didn't see the notification. The AI never misses a trigger.

### Innovation 3: The Karpathy Self-Improvement Loop
Every Friday at 6 PM, the system reviews the week's data:
- Which subject lines got the highest open rates?
- Which call openers led to meetings?
- Which SMS tones got responses?
- What pain points converted best?

It identifies the top and bottom 10% of performing messages, analyzes the patterns, and rewrites its own playbooks. Every week, the system gets measurably better. After 4 weeks, it's operating on strategies no human team would discover through manual A/B testing.
